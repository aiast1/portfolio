Hybrid Solar-Thermoelectric Power Generation

Lead Researcher 2022 — 2025 Samos, Greece

Overview

This research project investigates the development and evaluation of a hybrid solar-thermoelectric power generation system designed for marine environments, with usage of hydrodynamics propulsion. The work was published in the European Journal of Mechanical Engineering Research (2025) and won 3rd Place at the European Union Contest for Young Scientists (EUCYS) 2023 in Brussels.

Research Highlights

  • Designed and built a hybrid system combining solar panels with thermoelectric generators optimized for marine conditions
  • Investigated the use of hydrodynamics propulsion as part of the integrated energy system
  • Conducted experimental evaluation in real marine environment conditions

Recognition

  • 3rd Place, EUCYS 2023 — European Union Contest for Young Scientists, Brussels (€3,500 Prize)
  • 1st Place, Greek Young Scientists 2023 — Chosen as Best Research Project in Engineering to represent Greece at EUCYS
  • Won exam-free admission to any Greek university

Publication

Development and Evaluation of a Hybrid Solar-Thermoelectric Power Generation System in a Marine Environment and Usage of Hydrodynamics Propulsion
European Journal of Mechanical Engineering Research, Vol 12, Issue 1, 2025
